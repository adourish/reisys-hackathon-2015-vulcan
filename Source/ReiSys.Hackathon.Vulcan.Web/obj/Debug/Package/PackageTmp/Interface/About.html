Hackathon 2015

REI systems has been conducting hackathon annually since 2012 and developers strive to bring their ideas into implementation via this platform. There were many innovative ideas that have come through this platform and REI and its employees cherish this innovative cut throat competition on innovation.

This year, REI decided to encourage innovation in data and decision analytics by providing 3 publically available datasets and asking competitors to develop a concept and focus on innovation, technologies, usability and quality of code.

Project

Our project uses FEMAs disaster declaration datasets. The idea is simple – use the data to build visualizations for FEMA executives that can tell them Past Performance, Current Ongoing Projects and Prediction of what is coming.

History

Our History section shows FEMS executives and general public how FEMA has done in the past years, how much money it has cost for which types of disasters in what state.

Current

Our Current Section takes FEMA executives to a current FEMA dashboard https://itdashboard.gov/investment?buscid=165. Our idea was that FEMA executives will already have a dashboard designed to meet their current needs and more data than we have in publically available APIs. Hence we decided to keep this as a link that can take them to existing implementation.

Future

This forms the heart of our project. We invested significant time and resources to learn Machine Learning in Azure and trained the data to provide future predictions based on clustering. Our technology section will describe in detail our technology stack and the machine learning flow that we used to build the predictions.
